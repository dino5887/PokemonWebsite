What's a 'Gen?'

"Gen" is short for "generation" and refers to the time period in which a specific game was released.

  • 1st Gen - Pokemon Red, Blue, Yellow, and Green (Japan Only)
  • 2nd Gen - Pokemon Gold, Silver, and Crystal
  • 3rd Gen - Pokemon Ruby, Sapphire, and Emerald (Including Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire)
  • 4th Gen - Pokemon Diamond, Pearl, and Platinum
  • 5th Gen - Pokemon Black, White, Black 2, and White 2
  • 6th Gen - Pokemon X and Y
  • 7th Gen - Pokemon Sun and Moon
  • 8th Gen - Pokemon Sword and Shield

Each generation brought new features, new Pokemon, and added new ways for Pokemon to battle and for you to develop your relationship with them. But which one is the best to start with?

This is a completely subjective question, and there's really no wrong answer. The difficulty of the Pokemon series is geared so they can be enjoyed by fans of all ages, so most people who give the series a try won't find themselves in a position where they don't know what to do. Newer Pokemon games have features that make leveling Pokemon and other actions more convenient, but there's a lot to be said for starting with the basics.
